Fabric Department Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

This SOP defines the end-to-end fabric handling process for the Fabric Department, including order intake, pre-inward verification, inhouse fabric processing, out-of-house tracking, and quality assurance requirements.

1. Purpose

To establish a standardized process for fabric receipt, verification, inspection, storage, and reporting in the Fabric Department. This ensures consistent quality, compliance with customer requirements, and timely production readiness.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to all fabric materials received, inspected, and processed by the Fabric Department for production, including woven, knit, elastic, collar pieces, and associated accessories.

3. Responsibilities

  • Fabric Department Lead: Oversee implementation of the SOP and ensure all team members follow procedures.
  • Inward Coordinator: Verify documentation, inspect fabric deliveries, and update tracking records.
  • Quality Control (QC): Execute fabric inspection, shade matching, and acceptance decisions.
  • Sourcing Team: Provide accurate order details, delivery dates, and material specifications.
  • Production Planning: Communicate fabric requirements and safe stock needs.

4. Definitions

  • FPT Report: Fabric Performance Test report issued by the mill.
  • RFD: Ready for Dyeing fabric condition.
  • 4-Point Inspection: Fabric quality scoring based on defect severity.
  • Batch Number: Roll-wise identification used for traceability.

5. Fabric Order Process

  1. Receive Fabric Requisition (PR) via email from production planning with product details, quantities, and required delivery dates.
  2. Verify whether safety stock is required as part of the requisition.
  3. Obtain written approval from the Head Office (HO) or authorized buyer before placing the order.
  4. Confirm delivery schedule and material specifications with sourcing and the mill.

Key Control Points

  • All fabric orders must be documented in the order log with PR reference number.
  • No fabric should be ordered without formal approval from HO.

6. Pre-Inward Verification

Before allowing fabric into the facility, verify the following documents and conditions:

Document / Requirement Verification Action
Invoice Verify pricing, quantity, and supplier details.
Packing List Check roll counts, package details, and item descriptions.
Purchase Order (PO) Ensure PO matches the ordered fabric and delivery terms.
Challan Copy Confirm transporter and dispatch details.
Roll-wise Shrinkage Report Ensure shrinkage values are within acceptable limits.
FPT Report Verify fabric performance test results for compliance.
Roll-wise Batch Number Record batch numbers for traceability.

7. Fabric Inward Process

  1. Receive fabric and inspect all packages before unloading.
  2. Match received fabric details with invoice, packing list, PO, and challan.
  3. Verify roll-wise batch numbers and confirm them in the inward register.
  4. Reject or quarantine fabric if documentation is incomplete or inconsistent.
  5. Issue fabric to the relevant storage or processing location only after successful verification.

8. Inhouse Fabric Handling

For fabric that enters the inhouse process, follow these steps:

  1. Assign fabric to the correct style or collection and update the fabric blocking register.
  2. Perform roll-wise GSM and width measurement checks.
  3. Execute color matching against the approved lab dip.
  4. Complete the 4-point inspection report for each batch.
  5. Document acceptance or rejection in the inhouse fabric log.

Non-Conformance

If fabric rejection exceeds 10%, the material must be returned to the mill. Document the rejection reason, percentage rejected, and action taken.

9. Not Inhouse Tracking

For fabric not yet received or kept outside the inhouse process:

  • Track expected delivery dates style-wise and update the status tracker daily.
  • Maintain communication with the sourcing team and mill for delivery updates.
  • Share weekly status reports with stakeholders including production, sourcing, and quality teams.

10. Collar Specification Guidelines

Collars must be managed as part of the fabric inward and processing sequence:

  • Rib Collar: Inward along with body fabric, ensure stretch and recovery match the body fabric.
  • Y/D Flatknit Tipping Collar: Loose knit collar should be dyed with the bulk fabric, then rewound and issued for flatknit making.
  • Solid Dye Flatknit Collar: Film-dyed collar fabric in RFD condition should be issued for dyeing with the main fabric batch.

11. Quality Inspection

Use the 4-point inspection standard to evaluate fabric defects:

  1. Record total defect points from 1-point through 4-point defects.
  2. Calculate the score using the industry formula:
4-Point Score Formula: (Total Points × 3600) ÷ (Width in inches × Length in yards)

Accept fabrics with a score of 40 points or less per 100 square yards. Fabrics scoring above 40 are considered unacceptable.

12. Documentation and Reporting

  • Maintain an inward register with fabric details, batch numbers, and inspection results.
  • Record all approvals, deviations, and non-conformances in the department log.
  • Provide weekly updates to the management team on fabric status and pending actions.

Approved by Fabric Operations Head. This SOP is subject to periodic review and update.
